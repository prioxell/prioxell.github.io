Prioxell’s Formula for a Productive Life

Start with a Main List

Begin by identifying your life's goal or purpose. Write it down as the foundation of your to-do system. Define your purpose clearly, visualize it, and align your mindset to work towards it.

Short-Term Goals

After establishing your life's purpose, break it down into achievable short-term goals. Your daily to-do lists will focus on these. Ensure your short-term goals are connected to your life's larger purpose. This keeps you aligned and motivated. Blend these short-term goals with your day-to-day routine, using prioritization as the key and flexibility as the approach.

Prioritization Method

Prioritization is the process of ranking potential development items in order of importance. It should stem from your life goals. Purpose and time-awareness are the basic components. Always remember your goals and keep this mantra in mind:

  • Who you are
  • Where you are
  • What you are doing
  • Where you want to go
  • What obstacles lie in your way

Prioritization is not about being narcissistic. It’s about being someone who wants to grow and values both the journey and the destination.

Flexible Approach

The unpredictable nature of life and the necessity of adaptability is key. Flexibility allows you to focus on what truly matters, rather than being derailed by unexpected twists and turns.
